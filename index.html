About the teacher

My name is Iryna, and I have been living in the UK for the past 22 years. Originally from Ukraine, I am a native speaker of both Russian and Ukrainian.

In 1999, I graduated from Kharkiv State Pedagogical University in Ukraine, where I obtained an MA degree and qualified as a philologist, teacher of French, and translator.

In 2013, I completed a Postgraduate Certificate in Education (PGCE) in Modern Foreign Languages (French and Russian) in the UK.

I have many years of experience teaching French and Russian as foreign languages to students of different ages and proficiency levels. I offer lessons tailored to individual learning needs and goals. In addition, I have extensive experience conducting GCSE and A-Level speaking examinations at local schools.

Teaching philosophy

The goal is not only to help students learn Russian but also to inspire a lifelong interest in the language and the rich cultural heritage of the Russian-speaking world.

— Iryna Kurekina

A method built around how you actually learn

My method is built around how people naturally acquire languages. Instead of learning isolated words and rules, students encounter Russian in context through conversations, reading, listening activities, and real-life situations. New vocabulary and grammar are introduced gradually and reinforced through repetition, helping learners build confidence and retain what they learn. I focus on:
I
Communication first

Using Russian from the very beginning

II
Learning in context

Understanding words and grammar through meaningful examples.

III
Active practice

Speaking, listening, reading, and writing regularly.

IV
Spaced repetition

Reviewing material at the right time to improve long-term memory.

V
Cultural immersion

Learning about Russian culture alongside the language.

VI
Personalized progress

Allowing learners to advance at a pace that suits their goals and abilities.

By combining proven language-learning principles with engaging content and practical application, our approach helps students move beyond memorization and develop genuine fluency in Russian. The goal is simple: to make learning Russian effective, enjoyable, and sustainable.
